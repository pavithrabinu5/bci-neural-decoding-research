# High-Performance Brain-Computer Interfaces
### A Critical Evaluation of AI-Driven Neural Decoding Technologies

> Published as part of the Artificial Intelligence module  
> University of West London, RAK Branch Campus  
> Author: Pavithra Binu

---

## Abstract

This paper critically evaluates five landmark BCI studies spanning 2006–2023,
charting the evolution from foundational proof-of-concept demonstrations to 
clinically oriented speech and motor-restoration systems. Drawing on peer-reviewed 
research published in Nature and The Lancet, the analysis examines neural signal 
decoding, machine-learning architectures, and real-world functional performance.

**Key finding:** Communication speeds increased 4–5× between 2019 and 2023, driven 
by a paradigm shift from endpoint control to decoding of internal motor representations.

## ML Decoding Architecture Evolution

| Year | Algorithm | Key Characteristic |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| 2006 | Population Vector Algorithm | Linear, deterministic, 2D only |
| 2013 | Kalman Filter | Probabilistic, continuous trajectory |
| 2019 | Custom ASIC | Hardware-accelerated, scalable |
| 2021 | RNN / LSTM | Deep learning, high accuracy |
| 2023 | Bidirectional RNN + LLM | State-of-the-art, conversational speed |

---

## Critical Research Gaps Identified

- **Long-term signal stability** — no study shows stable performance beyond 5 years
- **Participant generalisation** — all human studies use a single participant
- **Bidirectional communication** — sensory feedback largely absent
- **Clinical deployment** — lab-to-life transition poorly characterised
- **Standardisation** — no unified performance metrics across studies
